Current Projects

Named Entity Recognition in Unstructured Medical Text Documents

Independent medical examiner reports contain personally identifiable information that needs to be removed or randomly encoded before further research work can be conducted. The goal of this research is to perform named entity recognition, a type of natural language processing, to identify and subsequently remove/encode Personally Identifiable Information from Independent Medical Examiner reports prepared by the physician. We apply the NER toolkits of OpenNLP and spaCy, two freely available Natural Language Processing platforms.

  • Presentation Upcoming at NCUR 2022 in Apr. 2022
  • Presented at ICECET 2021, Cape Town, South Africa in Dec. 2021
  • Research project at UW-Eau Claire, with Dr. Jim Seliya and Dr. Rushit Dave
